What is the 482 Skills in Demand (SID) Visa? Australia's main employer-sponsored temporary work visa. An Australian employer sponsors you to work in a specific role. You work for up to 4 years and can apply for permanent residency after 2 years. Replaced the old TSS 482 on 7 December 2024.

New Worker Protections (December 2024)
180-day portability — change employer without losing visa If your employer terminates you or you choose to leave, you have 180 days to find a new approved sponsor. During this period you can work any job. Previously this was only 60 days.
PR counts across multiple employers The 2 years of work needed before applying for 186 PR can now be accumulated across multiple approved sponsors — not just one employer. This removes the "trapped employee" problem.
SAF levy refund if you leave within 12 months If you leave Australia before 12 months, the employer can apply for a partial SAF levy refund. Refund rules unchanged from previous framework.
Employers cannot recover costs from employees It is illegal for an employer to pass the SAF levy, SBS fee, or nomination fee costs on to the visa holder. Penalties up to $93,000 per breach.

482 SID → Permanent Residency pathway (updated December 2024) The SID 482 is not permanent — but it is a structured stepping stone to PR. After 2 years of sponsored work you can apply for the Subclass 186 Employer Nomination Scheme (Permanent Residence). Work can now be spread across multiple approved sponsors.
Full Pathway — From Sponsorship to Permanent Residency
Start — Months 1–3
Employer Sponsorship Process
Your employer applies for Standard Business Sponsorship (SBS) — $420 fee, 2–3 weeks processing. Simultaneously lodges nomination for your position. Nomination fee $330 + SAF levy. Both can be lodged at same time — you do not need to wait for SBS before lodging nomination.
Month 1–8 (varies by stream)
Visa Application Processing
You lodge your visa application. Specialist Skills: 7–53 days. Core Skills: 4–8 months. Includes health check, police clearance, skills assessment (if required). Family members can be included — full work and study rights.
Month 1–8+
482 SID Visa Granted — Work Begins
You arrive in Australia (or continue working if onshore) in your nominated occupation. Visa is valid for up to 4 years. You work for your approved sponsor. Clock toward PR begins.
After 2 Years of Sponsored Work
186 PR Eligibility Unlocked
After 2 years of full-time sponsored work (reduced from 3 years), you become eligible to apply for the Subclass 186 Employer Nomination Scheme — permanent residency. The 2 years can be across multiple approved sponsors. You must be under 45 at time of nomination (some exceptions apply).
Month 24–36 — PR Application
186 ENS — Permanent Residency Granted
Your employer nominates you for permanent residency. You lodge the 186 visa application. Processing: typically 6–18 months. No points test — employer support is the key requirement. 92% success rate for TRT stream applicants in 2025.
Month 30–50+ (total journey)
Australian Permanent Resident
Permanent residency granted. You can live and work anywhere in Australia with any employer. After 4 years of permanent residency (1 year as citizen), you can apply for Australian citizenship.

Step-by-Step — Getting Sponsored
1

Confirm your occupation is on the CSOL

Check the official DHA CSOL list to confirm your ANZSCO occupation code is eligible. If you earn $141,210+, skip this — Specialist stream has no list requirement.

→ 1 day · Free at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au
2

Get your skills assessment (Core Skills only)

Most Core Skills occupations require a positive skills assessment before the employer lodges your nomination. Start early — Engineers Australia, CPA, AHPRA, ACS, VETASSESS etc. Takes 2–4 months.

→ 2–4 months · $500–$1,000
3

Find an employer willing to sponsor

Target employers who already have SBS approval — they're faster and more familiar with the process. Use LinkedIn to filter "Visa Sponsorship" in Australia. Target industries with genuine shortages (healthcare, tech, engineering, trades). Be upfront about needing sponsorship early in the conversation.

→ Timeline varies · Research employers at visatasker.com for recently active sponsors
4

Employer lodges Standard Business Sponsorship (SBS)

If not already an approved sponsor, your employer applies for SBS first — $420 fee, 2–3 weeks processing. They can lodge the nomination at the same time — no need to wait.

→ 2–3 weeks · $420 (employer pays)
5

Employer lodges nomination

Employer nominates you for the specific position. Must demonstrate Labour Market Testing (genuine attempt to find an Australian first — usually 28 days of advertising). Nomination fee $330 + SAF levy.

→ 1–3 months · SAF levy $1,200–$7,200 (employer pays, cannot be passed to you)
6

You lodge visa application

Once nomination is lodged (does not need approval first), you can lodge your visa application. Gather: passport, English test results, skills assessment, health check, police clearances, employment evidence.

→ Specialist: 7–53 days · Core Skills: 4–8 months · $3,210 (you pay)
7

Visa granted — work begins

You work for your approved sponsor in the nominated occupation. Keep records of your employment dates — this is evidence for your 186 PR application. You need 2 years of full-time work before applying for PR.

→ Up to 4 years · Clock toward PR starts

Common Rejection Reasons — Avoid These
Salary below market rate — Your offer must meet both the TSMIT ($76,515) AND the market salary rate for your occupation. DHA checks both. Low-balled offers are a leading cause of nomination refusal.
Inadequate Labour Market Testing — Employer must demonstrate genuine attempts to hire locally (typically 28 days advertising). Weak LMT is the second most common refusal reason.
Skills assessment not matching occupation — Your skills assessment must be in the exact nominated occupation code. Mismatched ANZSCO codes cause refusals.
Insufficient work experience evidence — Employment references, payslips, tax records. 1 year minimum within last 5 years. Missing documentation is the most common delay cause.
Expired English test results — English tests are valid for 3 years. Check your test date before lodging.
Sponsoring a worker — what you need to know as an employer You must be approved as a Standard Business Sponsor. You pay the SAF levy and cannot recover it from the employee. The process has three stages that can largely run in parallel.
Employer Obligations — Ongoing
Pay market salary rate — You must pay the higher of the TSMIT ($76,515) or the market salary rate for the occupation and location. DHA conducts salary audits.
Do not recover costs — You cannot ask the worker to reimburse SAF levy, SBS, or nomination fees. Fine up to $93,000 per breach.
Notify DHA of changes — If the worker's role changes significantly, you may need to lodge a new nomination. Termination must be notified.
Keep employment records — DHA can audit your sponsorship at any time. Keep payslips, employment contracts and job descriptions.
Labour Market Testing validity — LMT results are valid for 4 months. If the nomination is not lodged within 4 months, you may need to re-advertise.
Step-by-Step — Sponsoring a Worker
1

Apply for Standard Business Sponsorship (SBS)

Lodge via ImmiAccount. Provide business registration (ABN), Business Activity Statements, commitment to Australian employment law. Valid for 5 years once approved.

→ 2 weeks – 4 months · $420
2

Conduct Labour Market Testing (LMT)

Advertise the position genuinely for at least 28 days on Australian job platforms (Seek, LinkedIn, industry platforms). Keep all records — number of applicants, why none were suitable. LMT is valid for 4 months.

→ Minimum 28 days · Can run alongside SBS application
3

Lodge nomination for the worker

Nominate the specific worker for the specific position. Pay SAF levy upfront ($1,200–$1,800/year of visa × visa years). Include position description, salary evidence, LMT results.

→ 1–4 months processing · $330 + SAF levy
4

Worker lodges visa application

Worker can lodge as soon as nomination is lodged (not approved). They prepare their own documents — English test, skills assessment, health check, police clearances.

→ Worker-managed · $3,210 worker pays
5

Visa approved — manage ongoing compliance

Keep employment records. Pay market salary. Notify DHA of any changes. When the worker reaches 2 years, you can nominate them for 186 permanent residency if you wish to retain them permanently.

→ Ongoing · 186 PR nomination at 2-year mark

Visa Condition 8607 — The Master Rule
⚠️ This condition applies to every primary 482 holder. Breaching it can result in visa cancellation under s116 of the Migration Act.
Rule 1 — Work only in your nominated occupation
You must perform the duties of the exact ANZSCO occupation your visa was granted for. If your job title changes or your duties shift significantly — even at the same employer — this may breach condition 8607. A Software Engineer cannot perform ICT Manager duties without a new nomination.
Rule 2 — Work only for your approved sponsor
You must work for the business that sponsored you — or an associated entity of that Australian business. You cannot work for a competitor or unrelated employer while employed by your sponsor. Exception: during the 180-day portability window (see below), you can work any job.
Rule 3 — Start work within 90 days
You must start working for your sponsor within 90 days of your visa being granted (if you were onshore) or within 90 days of first arriving in Australia (if you were offshore). Missing this deadline is a breach.
Rule 4 — Hold required licences within 90 days
If your occupation requires a licence or registration (e.g. AHPRA for nurses, state teaching registration for teachers, electrical licence for electricians) you must obtain it within 90 days of starting work and keep it current. If your licence is refused or cancelled, notify DHA immediately and stop performing duties requiring that licence.

Changing Employers — Step by Step
The 180-day portability rule (new Dec 2024) — If you lose your job or resign, you have 180 days to find a new approved sponsor. During this time you can work any job to support yourself. Maximum total time without compliant sponsorship across the whole visa: 365 days.
Scenario A — Same occupation, new employer
1

Employment ends — 180-day clock starts

From the day your employment with your sponsor ceases, the 180-day portability window begins. You can work any job during this time. Your old employer must notify DHA of the cessation via ImmiAccount.

2

New employer lodges nomination for you

Your new employer must be an approved Standard Business Sponsor (or become one — $420, 2–3 weeks). They lodge a new nomination for you in the same ANZSCO occupation. You do NOT need a new visa grant if the occupation is identical.

→ Nomination processing: 1–4 months
3

Wait for nomination approval before starting

⚠️ Critical — you cannot start working for the new employer in your nominated occupation until the new nomination is approved. Starting before approval breaches condition 8607. You can continue working in any other job during the wait.

4

Begin work — PR clock continues

Once the nomination is approved, start work immediately. Your PR clock continues from your previous 482 employment — the 2 years counts across multiple approved sponsors.

Scenario B — Different occupation, new employer
If your new role is a different ANZSCO occupation code from your current nomination, you need both a new nomination AND a new 482 visa application. You cannot start the new occupation until the new visa is granted. This takes 4–8 months for Core Skills stream.

Travelling While on the 482
Travel freely in and out of Australia
The 482 SID visa allows multiple entries. You can travel internationally and re-enter Australia freely for the full duration of the visa — no travel facility expiry to worry about (unlike some visas where travel rights expire separately).

Check your visa expiry before booking travel
If your 482 expires while you are overseas, you cannot re-enter on that visa. Always check your visa validity at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au before travelling and ensure any new visa or bridging visa is in place if needed.
Health Insurance — Mandatory Requirement
Health insurance is not optional. All 482 primary and secondary visa holders must maintain adequate health insurance for the entire visa period. Failure to maintain it is a breach of visa conditions.
What counts as "adequate"
A recognised private health insurance policy covering hospital and medical treatment in Australia. Must cover all visa holders named in the application.
Reciprocal healthcare exemptions
Citizens of UK, Ireland, New Zealand, Sweden, Netherlands, Belgium, Finland, Italy, Malta, Norway and Slovenia may access Medicare and may be exempt. Check the current reciprocal healthcare agreement list at the Department of Health website.

If Your Application is Refused — What Happens
38% of 482 refusals are nomination refusals — meaning the employer's nomination failed, which automatically triggers a visa refusal. A refusal is not always final.
Step 1 — You have 21 calendar days to appeal
From the date of the refusal letter, you have exactly 21 days to lodge a Merits Review application with the Administrative Review Tribunal (ART). This deadline is absolute — missing it removes your right to review. If you are onshore at the time of refusal, you will typically be granted a Bridging Visa A (BVA) allowing you to remain in Australia while the review is processed.
Step 2 — Merits Review at the ART
The Administrative Review Tribunal takes a fresh look at your case. You can provide new and updated evidence that was not in your original application. Success rate is approximately 32% when fresh evidence is supplied. The ART cannot grant the visa itself — a successful review sends the case back to DHA for reconsideration.
Step 3 — Other options if review is unsuccessful
Ministerial Intervention — For compelling or compassionate circumstances. Not guaranteed and rarely granted.

Judicial Review (Federal Circuit Court) — For legal errors only, not factual disagreements.

Re-lodge from offshore — If the underlying issues are fixed (e.g. employer improves their LMT, nomination is re-drafted), a fresh application can be lodged from outside Australia.
Section 48 bar — important onshore restriction: If you are in Australia on a Bridging Visa after a refusal, Section 48 of the Migration Act prevents you from lodging most other visa types onshore. Limited exceptions include: Partner visa (820/801), some Skilled visas (190/491/494), and Protection visas.
Visa Cancellation — What Can Trigger It
Breaching Condition 8607 — Working outside your occupation or for an unauthorised employer. DHA can cancel under Section 116 of the Migration Act.
Your sponsor's sponsorship is cancelled — If DHA bars or cancels your employer's Standard Business Sponsorship, this directly affects your visa status.
Character or health failure — Conviction for a serious offence, or failure to maintain required health standards.
Providing false information — Any misrepresentation in your application — documents, employment history, qualifications — is grounds for immediate cancellation.
If cancelled: You have the right to a Merits Review (if cancelled inside Australia). You must depart within the period stated on the cancellation notice. A cancelled visa creates a 3-year re-entry ban in most circumstances.
Tax and Medicare — Quick Facts
Tax residency is separate from visa status
You can be a tax resident of Australia on a temporary visa. Tax residency is determined by your intentions and circumstances — not your visa subclass. Most 482 holders working full-time in Australia are treated as tax residents and taxed at resident rates (more favourable than non-resident rates). Confirm your status with an Australian accountant.
Medicare Levy Surcharge
If you earn above $93,000 (single) and do not have private hospital cover, you may be subject to the Medicare Levy Surcharge (1–1.5% of income) even as a temporary visa holder. Private health insurance often costs less than the surcharge.
Superannuation
