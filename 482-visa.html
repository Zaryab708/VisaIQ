While You're on the 482 Visa — Your Rights
✅ You CAN
  • Work full-time for your sponsor
  • Bring family on secondary visa
  • Travel in and out of Australia freely
  • Study part-time
  • Change employer (with 180-day window)
  • Apply for 186 after 2 years
❌ You CANNOT
  • Work for a non-sponsoring employer (without portability period)
  • Access Medicare (unless bilateral agreement)
  • Vote in elections
  • Work in a different ANZSCO occupation (need new visa)
  • Stay if visa cancelled without finding new sponsor within 180 days
The 180-day portability rule: Since December 2024, if your employment ends you have 180 days to find a new approved sponsor. During this time you can work any job. After 180 days your visa is cancelled if no new nomination is lodged. This replaced the old 60-day rule.
Condition 8607: You must notify the Department of Home Affairs if your employment ends. Failure to notify within 28 days is a visa condition breach — even if you find a new sponsor.
Frequently Asked Questions
What is the 482 visa salary threshold in 2026?
The 482 visa minimum salary threshold (TSMIT) is $76,515 for the Core Skills stream in 2026. The Specialist Skills stream requires at least $141,210 per year. These figures are indexed annually on 1 July. Your employer must pay at or above the TSMIT — and at least the market salary rate for your occupation — for their nomination to be approved.
How long does a 482 visa take to process in 2026?
Under the new UDPE processing system (live 28 March 2026), the target processing time is 15 days for Core Skills and 7 days for Specialist Skills. In practice, applications with complete documentation are being processed within this window. Complex cases, character issues, or incomplete documents will take longer.
Can I get permanent residency on a 482 visa?
Yes. After 2 years of sponsored work in Australia, you can apply for the Subclass 186 (Employer Nomination Scheme) permanent residence visa via the Temporary Residence Transition (TRT) stream. The 2-year requirement was reduced from 3 years in December 2024. You must be under 45 when your employer lodges the 186 nomination. The 2 years can be with multiple approved sponsors — it does not need to be with one employer.
Can I change employers on a 482 visa?
Yes. Since December 2024 you have 180 days to find a new approved sponsor if you leave your employer. During this portability window you can work any job. Your new employer must lodge a fresh nomination. If you change to a different ANZSCO occupation code, you also need a new 482 visa application. Always notify the Department within 28 days of your employment ending (condition 8607).
Do I need a skills assessment for the 482 visa?
For the Core Skills stream, a skills assessment is required for most occupations. Your relevant assessing body depends on your occupation — ANMAC for nurses, Engineers Australia or EA for engineers, ACS for ICT, AHPRA for health practitioners, TRA for trades, AITSL for teachers. For the Specialist Skills stream (salary $141,210+), no skills assessment is required.
What replaced the TSS visa in Australia?
The Skills in Demand (SID) visa replaced the Temporary Skill Shortage (TSS) visa on 7 December 2024. The subclass number (482) remained the same. The new visa introduced three streams — Specialist Skills, Core Skills, and Essential Skills — replacing the old Short-Term and Medium-Term stream structure. Key improvements: 2-year TRT pathway (down from 3), 180-day employer change window (up from 60 days), reduced experience requirement (1 year down from 2).
How much does a 482 visa cost in total?
Your total out-of-pocket cost (excluding optional migration agent) is approximately $10,500 – $13,000 including skills assessment ($500-$1,000), visa application fee ($4,640), health examination ($300-$500), police clearances ($50-$300), and English test ($420-$490). Your employer pays the nomination fee ($330), SAF levy ($1,200-$1,800/year), and SBS application ($420) if not already approved. The 186 PR application adds another ~$4,640 in visa fees.
What is the SAF levy and who pays it?
The Skilling Australians Fund (SAF) levy is a training contribution paid by the employer — not you. It is $1,200 per year for businesses with turnover under $10 million (small business) and $1,800 per year for larger businesses. It must be paid upfront for the full visa period. For a 2-year 482 visa, a small employer pays $2,400 and a large employer pays $3,600 upfront. This is a significant cost to the employer and worth understanding when negotiating sponsorship.
