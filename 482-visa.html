What is the 482 Skills in Demand (SID) Visa? Australia's main employer-sponsored temporary work visa. An Australian employer sponsors you to work in a specific role for up to 4 years. You can apply for permanent residency after 2 years. Replaced the old TSS 482 on 7 December 2024. Minimum salary (TSMIT) is $73,150 for Core Skills in 2026.

New Worker Protections (December 2024)
180-day portability — change employer without losing visa If your employer terminates you or you choose to leave, you have 180 days to find a new approved sponsor. During this period you can work any job. Previously this was only 60 days.
PR counts across multiple employers The 2 years of work needed before applying for 186 PR can now be accumulated across multiple approved sponsors — not just one employer. This removes the "trapped employee" problem.
SAF levy refund if you leave within 12 months If you leave Australia before 12 months, the employer can apply for a partial SAF levy refund.
Employers cannot recover costs from employees It is illegal for an employer to pass the SAF levy, SBS fee, or nomination fee costs on to the visa holder. Penalties up to $93,000 per breach.
How occupation eligibility works for the SID 482 visa For the Core Skills stream, your occupation must appear on the Core Skills Occupation List (CSOL). For the Specialist Skills stream, any professional occupation qualifies if you earn $141,210+. Trades, machine operators and labourers cannot access the Specialist stream.

Occupation Caveats — 14 Special Conditions

Some CSOL occupations have additional requirements called "caveats." Even if your occupation is on the list, you must also meet the caveat to be eligible.

Registered Nurse — must hold AHPRA registration or have applied for it
Medical Practitioners — must hold or be eligible for specialist registration
Engineering occupations — may require Engineers Australia assessment
Accountants — must have CPA/CA ANZ/IPA assessment
Teachers — must hold or be eligible for state teaching registration
+ 9 other occupation-specific caveats. Always check the official DHA caveat list for your ANZSCO code.
482 SID → Permanent Residency pathway (updated December 2024) The SID 482 is not permanent — but it is a structured stepping stone to PR. After 2 years of sponsored work you can apply for the Subclass 186 Employer Nomination Scheme. Work can be spread across multiple approved sponsors. Full Pathway — From Sponsorship to Permanent Residency
Start — Months 1–3
Employer Sponsorship Process
Your employer applies for Standard Business Sponsorship (SBS) — $420 fee, 2–3 weeks processing. Simultaneously lodges nomination for your position. Nomination fee $330 + SAF levy. Both can be lodged at same time.
15 days target (UDPE system — live 28 March 2026)
Visa Application Processing
You lodge your visa application. Specialist Skills: 7–53 days. Core Skills: 15-day target under the new UDPE system (launched 28 March 2026). Includes health check, police clearance, skills assessment (if required). Family members can be included — full work and study rights.
Month 1+
482 SID Visa Granted — Work Begins
You work for your approved sponsor in your nominated occupation. Visa is valid for up to 4 years. Clock toward PR begins.
After 2 Years of Sponsored Work
186 PR Eligibility Unlocked
After 2 years of full-time sponsored work (reduced from 3 years), you become eligible to apply for the Subclass 186 Employer Nomination Scheme. The 2 years can be across multiple approved sponsors. You must be under 45 at time of nomination.
Month 24–42 — PR Grant
186 ENS — Permanent Residency Granted
Your employer nominates you for permanent residency. You lodge the 186 visa application ($4,770 fee). Processing: 6–18 months. No points test — employer support is the key requirement.

Visa Condition 8607 — The Master Rule
⚠️ This condition applies to every primary 482 holder. Breaching it can result in visa cancellation under s116 of the Migration Act.
Rule 1 — Work only in your nominated occupation
You must perform the duties of the exact ANZSCO occupation your visa was granted for. If your job title changes or your duties shift significantly — even at the same employer — this may breach condition 8607.
Rule 2 — Work only for your approved sponsor
You must work for the business that sponsored you or an associated entity. Exception: during the 180-day portability window, you can work any job.
Rule 3 — Start work within 90 days
You must start working for your sponsor within 90 days of your visa being granted (onshore) or within 90 days of first arriving in Australia (offshore).
Rule 4 — Hold required licences within 90 days
If your occupation requires a licence or registration (AHPRA for nurses, state teaching registration for teachers, etc.) you must obtain it within 90 days of starting work and keep it current.
Work Rights — What You Can and Cannot Do
✓ You CAN
Work full-time for your sponsor in your nominated occupation
Work for an associated entity of your Australian sponsor
Work any job during the 180-day portability window
Study at any Australian institution (no government aid)
Travel freely in and out of Australia while visa is valid
Apply for points-tested visas (189/190) simultaneously
Include partner and children — full work and study rights
✗ You CANNOT
Work for a different employer while sponsored (outside portability window)
Perform duties outside your nominated ANZSCO occupation
Work a second job for another employer (unless in 180-day window)
Access Medicare (unless reciprocal health agreement applies)
Access Centrelink or most government financial assistance
Stay beyond visa expiry without a new or bridging visa
Changing Employers — The 180-Day Rule
The 180-day portability rule (new Dec 2024) — If you lose your job or resign, you have 180 days to find a new approved sponsor. During this time you can work any job. Maximum total time without compliant sponsorship across the whole visa: 365 days.
1

Employment ends — 180-day clock starts

From the day your employment with your sponsor ceases, the 180-day portability window begins. You can work any job during this time. Your old employer must notify DHA of the cessation via ImmiAccount.

2

New employer lodges nomination for you

Your new employer must be an approved Standard Business Sponsor. They lodge a new nomination for you in the same ANZSCO occupation. You do NOT need a new visa grant if the occupation is identical.

→ Nomination processing: 1–4 months under UDPE
3

Wait for nomination approval before starting

⚠️ You cannot start working for the new employer in your nominated occupation until the new nomination is approved. Starting before approval breaches condition 8607.

4

Begin work — PR clock continues

Once the nomination is approved, start work. Your PR clock continues — the 2 years counts across multiple approved sponsors.
