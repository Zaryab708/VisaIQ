Section 1
Student visa requirements
📋 Core requirements — subclass 500
Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)Required from a CRICOS-registered provider before applying. Your provider issues this after you pay your fees.
Genuine Student (GS) requirementReplaced the old GTE statement. You must demonstrate that studying in Australia is consistent with your circumstances and that you intend to comply with visa conditions.
English proficiencyIELTS, TOEFL, PTE, or Cambridge. Minimum scores vary by provider. Most universities require IELTS 6.0–6.5 overall.
Financial capacityMust show sufficient funds — approximately AUD $24,505 for the first year of study plus living costs and travel.
Health insurance (OSHC)Overseas Student Health Cover is mandatory for the full duration of your visa. Must be purchased before visa grant.
Health examinationRequired for most applicants from high-risk countries for tuberculosis and other conditions. Results valid for 12 months.
Character requirementsMust provide police clearance certificates from countries lived in for 12+ months in the past 10 years.
⚠️ Genuine Student requirement — EL3 assessment level
DHA introduced EL3 (Enhanced Level 3) assessments for some nationalities from September 2023. This requires additional documentation demonstrating your ties to your home country, your financial situation and your genuine intention to study. If your nationality triggers EL3, expect a more detailed application and potentially longer processing. The tool above shows your evidence level.

Section 3
Student visa work rights
⏱ Work rights — DHA Condition 8105
During your course (in session)
Maximum 48 hours per fortnight while your course is in session — applies during all teaching periods and exam periods. Applies to all jobs combined. One fortnight = 14 days starting on a Monday.
During official course breaks and holidays
Unlimited work hours when your course is not in session. Semester breaks, summer holidays, mid-semester breaks. The 48-hour cap resumes when your next teaching period starts.
Masters by Research / PhD
No work hour restriction at any time once your course has commenced. Applies to research-stream degrees only — confirm via VEVO.
The July 2023 change — The cap increased from 40 to 48 hours per fortnight during study periods. The course break exemption (unlimited hours) was not removed. Unlimited hours during official breaks still applies. Source: DHA Condition 8105
What counts as workAll paid employment across all employers. Unpaid work that would normally attract payment. Does NOT include mandatory CRICOS-registered placements.
Your partner's work rightsBachelor or lower: partner limited to 48 hrs/fortnight. Masters or PhD: partner has unlimited work rights.
Breach consequenceVisa cancellation. Always track hours across a rolling 14-day period — not just week by week.
Section 4
Genuine Student requirement
🎓 What DHA assesses under GS

The Genuine Student requirement replaced the old Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) statement. DHA assesses whether your intention to study is genuine and whether you are likely to comply with visa conditions. It is not just about whether you will go home — it is about whether studying in Australia makes sense for your circumstances.

Value of the courseDoes the qualification add value to your career? Is it consistent with your educational history?
Home country circumstancesYour employment prospects, family ties, assets, and financial situation at home.
Immigration historyPrevious visa compliance, travel history, previous study visa applications.
Potential immigration advantageDHA considers whether the course could lead to a permanent migration advantage — this does not automatically disqualify you but must be addressed honestly.
Your statementA clear, honest explanation of why you are choosing this course, this provider, and Australia specifically.
Common refusal reason — A generic statement that does not address your personal circumstances. Your GS statement must be specific to you — not a template. If your nationality triggers EL3, invest time in a detailed, personalised statement.

Section 6
Student visa to Australian PR
🎯 The complete student to PR pathway
500 Student → Graduate → 485 Work visa → Submit EOI → 189/190/491 PR
485 Post-Study Work visa2–4 years work rights after graduation. Duration depends on degree level and study location. Apply within 6 months of completing your course.
Skills assessmentRequired for skilled migration. Start during your final year of study — takes 3–12 months. Check your assessing authority at occupation-demand.
NAATI CCL bonus5 extra DHA points. Available for Urdu, Hindi, Nepali, Tagalog, Bengali, Vietnamese, Mandarin and more. $800, 3 months. Do it before you graduate.
Regional study bonus+5 points for studying at a regional institution. Combined with 491 nomination (+15 pts) = 20-point advantage. See the calculator →
189 / 190 / 491 PR visaApply via SkillSelect EOI. Timeline depends on occupation, points, and state. Healthcare occupations average 5–6 months. ICT and accounting can take 18–24 months.
