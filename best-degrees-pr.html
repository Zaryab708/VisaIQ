What to do if your degree is in the "Difficult" category
Option 1 — SA state nomination (most practical)
South Australia accepts accounting, IT and business graduates at 65 base points for 190/491 nomination — where NSW and VIC will not. Adelaide is liveable, affordable and has a large South Asian community. SA is the most practical answer for most accountants and ICT managers.
Option 2 — Employer sponsorship (482 → 186)
For ICT, accounting and business graduates, finding an employer who will sponsor a 482 visa and then nominate for 186 after 2 years is often more realistic than competing in the 189 queue. Build the relationship during your 485 period.
Option 3 — Postgraduate in a high-demand field
A postgrad qualification in nursing, allied health or education combined with your existing degree can significantly improve your visa options. Graduate Diploma of Education (Secondary) for example opens Tier 2 pathways if you have a relevant bachelor's.
Option 4 — NAATI CCL + Superior English + 491 regional
For accountants and ICT professionals: NAATI CCL (+5 pts) + Superior English (+10 pts vs proficient) + 491 regional (+15 pts) = potentially 30 extra points on a difficult profile. This combination can turn an uncompetitive 189 profile into a viable 491 SA pathway.
