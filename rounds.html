189 Round 2 — 2025–26 · 10,000 Invitations · New Tier System Results
The second 189 SkillSelect invitation round for the 2025–26 program year issued 10,000 invitations across priority occupations. Combined with the August 2025 round (6,887 invitations), total 2025–26 invitations reached 16,887 — approaching the annual planning level. Trades and healthcare received the most invitations at the lowest cutoffs. ICT and accounting remained highly competitive with cutoffs at 90–95 points.

189 Round 1 — 2025–26 · First Round Under New Quarterly System
The first 189 SkillSelect invitation round under the new quarterly schedule for 2025–26. The round introduced the four-tier occupation prioritisation system for the first time — healthcare (Tier 1) and education (Tier 2) received the most invitations relative to their EOI pool size. Smaller than the November round at 6,887 invitations total.

NSW 190 Invitation Round — March 2026
NSW announced a Subclass 190 invitation round for the first week of March 2026. Applicants were required to have their SkillSelect EOI updated and lodged by COB Sunday 1 March 2026. NSW uses merit and sector-based selection — no published points cutoff. Priority sectors: Construction, Renewable Energy, Digital and Cyber, Care Economy.
