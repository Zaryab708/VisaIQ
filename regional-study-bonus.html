Regional Study Bonus
Explained

Study at a regional institution (+5 pts) then get regional nomination for 491 visa (+15 pts). Together that is a 20-point advantage over someone who studied in Sydney or Melbourne.

+5
Australian Study Requirement (Regional)
Added to your DHA points score if you completed at least 2 years of study at a CRICOS-registered institution in a regional area of Australia. This bonus stays on your points score permanently once earned.
Requirements:
✓ 2 academic years minimum at regional institution
✓ CRICOS-registered course
✓ Physically in regional area during study
✓ Claimed in your EOI
+15
491 Regional Nomination
Added to your effective points score when a state or territory nominates you for the 491 regional visa. This is the single biggest points boost in the entire DHA system. Requires living and working in regional Australia for 3 years then you can apply for 191 PR.
Requirements:
✓ State or territory nominates you
✓ Eligible occupation on MLTSSL
✓ Skills assessment required
✓ Must live regional 3 years after grant
Combined total
+20 points

The Hidden Bonus — Second 485 Visa for Regional Study
This is one of the most underused advantages in the Australian migration system. If you studied at a regional institution and hold a Post-Higher Education Work (485) visa, you may be eligible for a SECOND 485 visa adding 1–2 additional years in Australia.
What it gives you
+1–2 extra years
On top of your original 2–3 year 485 visa. More time to build points and work experience.
Who qualifies
Regional study
Must have completed at least 1 academic year in a regional area of Australia (not limited to 2 years).
Practical example: A nursing student who studies at University of Tasmania (Hobart) for 3 years: gets +5 regional study points permanently, qualifies for a second 485 (extra 1–2 years), is eligible for TAS 491 regional nomination (+15 pts) AND has access to TAS state nomination (one of the most accessible in Australia). Total potential points advantage over a Sydney student with identical academic results: 20+ points.
Best States for the Combined +20 Points Strategy
South Australia — Best overall for most occupations
Rank #1
All of SA including Adelaide is regional-eligible. UniSA, Flinders and University of Adelaide all give +5 regional study bonus. SA 190 at 65 pts. SA 491 at 65 pts. Accepts accountants — which NSW/VIC will not. Large South Asian community. Lower cost of living than Sydney/Melbourne. This is the single best state for most Pakistani and Indian students.
ACT (Canberra) — Best for IT and government roles
Rank #2
All of ACT is regional. ANU and University of Canberra give +5 regional study bonus. Strong tech sector (government IT). ACT 491 processes fast (3–6 months). Canberra has the lowest unemployment in Australia. Best city for government IT and cybersecurity professionals.
Tasmania — Fastest processing, healthcare focus
Rank #3
All of TAS is regional. University of Tasmania gives +5 study bonus. Weekly nomination rounds (fastest of any state). 65 pt cutoff. Must live in TAS for 6–9 months first. Best for healthcare, teaching and IT professionals wanting the fastest possible PR timeline.
