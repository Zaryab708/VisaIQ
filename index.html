How do I check my position in the SkillSelect EOI backlog?
Log into ImmiAccount to see your EOI status. To understand how many applicants sit above you in the queue, use the VisaIQ EOI Backlog Tracker — it shows pool size and points distribution for 52 occupations using April 2026 DHA data, so you can estimate your percentile without waiting for an invitation round.
What is the current 189 points cutoff for Registered Nurses in 2026?
The 189 federal cutoff for Registered Nurses (General) held at 75 points across four consecutive rounds from June 2024 to April 2026. SA 190 invites nurses at a 65-point minimum — the most accessible pathway for healthcare workers in 2026. Check the live tracker →
How many points do I need for Australian skilled PR in 2026?
The minimum to submit an EOI is 65 points, but invitation cutoffs vary significantly by occupation. Electricians were invited at 65 points via 189 in April 2026. Nurses require 75 points. Early Childhood Teachers require 85 points. Calculate your score →
Which state is easiest for 190 nomination in 2026?
South Australia (SA) is consistently the most accessible 190 pathway in 2026. SA runs monthly rounds, accepts applicants at 65 points minimum for Tier 1 occupations, and allows onshore processing. Tasmania also runs monthly rounds with 65-point minimums. Compare all 8 states →
How long is the SkillSelect EOI wait for my occupation?
Wait time depends entirely on your occupation, points score, and visa stream. Frozen occupations (Software Engineers, Accountants) have not received 189 invitations in over 12 months. Active occupations (Nurses, Electricians) above the cutoff are typically invited within 1–3 rounds. Check your occupation's backlog →
How is the 482 TSMIT salary threshold changing in July 2026?
The Temporary Skilled Migration Income Threshold (TSMIT) rises from $76,515 to $79,499 on 1 July 2026. All 482 employer sponsored applicants must meet this threshold. Check your 482 salary eligibility before 30 June →
